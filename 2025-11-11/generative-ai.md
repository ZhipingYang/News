This is based on the 80/20 rule, the idea that 20% of what you learn gives you 80% of the results.

You'll instantly get a clear roadmap, major concepts, and key resources.

Most people spend hours scrolling through random YouTube videos or blog posts trying to find the "best" resources.
You don't have to.
Ask ChatGPT this: "Give me the best learning resources (books, videos, podcasts, courses) for [topic]. Make sure they fit different learning styles, especially visual learners".
In seconds, you'll have a curated list of quality resources, like someone already did the research for you.

This one's a life-saver.
Sometimes, you read something like a technical concept, a financial term, or an academic paper, and your brain just goes blank.
Here's where ChatGPT shines:
Prompt: "Explain [concept] like I'm 5".

Or prompt like: "Summarize this text into bullet points of key ideas and insights: [paste your text]".
In seconds, it breaks down complex topics into simple language. You can even ask it to give analogies, examples, or visuals to make it stick.

Prompt: "Pretend to be an SEO expert. Audit my website and suggest fixes to improve rankings".
